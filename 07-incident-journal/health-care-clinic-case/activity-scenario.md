## Scenario
A small U.S. health care clinic specializing in primary-care services experienced a security incident on a Tuesday morning at approximately **9:00 a.m.** Several employees reported that they were unable to use their computers to access files such as **medical records**. Business operations shut down because employees could not access the files and software needed to do their jobs.

Employees also reported that a **ransom note** was displayed on their computers. The note stated that all company files were **encrypted** by an organized group of unethical hackers known to target organizations in the **healthcare** and **transportation** industries. The note demanded a large sum of money in exchange for the **decryption key**.

The attackers gained access to the company network by using **targeted phishing emails** sent to several employees. The phishing emails contained a **malicious attachment** that installed malware on an employee’s computer once it was downloaded.

After gaining access, the attackers deployed **ransomware**, which encrypted critical files. The organization was unable to access critical patient data, causing major disruptions to business operations. The company shut down its computer systems and contacted external organizations to report the incident and request technical assistance.

## Key details (for documentation)
- **Organization:** Small U.S. health care clinic (primary care)
- **When:** Tuesday, ~9:00 a.m.
- **Impact:** Employees unable to access files/software; operations shut down
- **Indicator:** Ransom note displayed; files encrypted
- **Initial access:** Targeted phishing emails with malicious attachment
- **Attack type:** Ransomware deployment and file encryption
- **Business outcome:** Loss of access to patient data; systems shut down; incident reported for assistance
